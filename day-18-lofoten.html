NORTHCAPE 2024

DAY 18 - LOFOTEN

Sandoya – Henningsvær – Stamsund – Mortsund – Leknes – Sandoya (163 km)

It’s time for a change of plans. Not that we had actually had a fixed one in our minds when we were considering to do Reinebringen again today. But the thought had been quite appealing yesterday. Not wanting to risk being greeted again by a sulky and foggy top, however, we decide to go the other direction to Henningsvær after having sent Julie off to Leknes airport for her flight back to the UK.

This time I manage to climb the top of Festvågtinden while Brigitta decides on the scenic opt-out version and stays at a little lake almost half-way. It’s a wise decision as the bit from the lake to the top isn’t for the faint-hearted. And the fact that the wind has picked up to gale-force gusts doesn’t help on the ridge with vertical drops.

Having survived the ordeal, and after being reunited with at least one of my fellow Nordic nymphs, we make our way down to the car and drive into Henningsvær, also known as the little Venice of the Lofoten as it consists of many small islands that are linked by bridges. It also praises itself to have one of the most famous football fields in the world. Check out the picture to know why.

We stock up on calories in the form of a nice coffee & cake stop, then set out on our scenic drive back via two small peninsulas to the Southwest of Leknes. In Leknes itself we have our first evening meal in a restaurant, then buy our last provisions for tomorrow and head back home. The sauna is a Must after this long day.
