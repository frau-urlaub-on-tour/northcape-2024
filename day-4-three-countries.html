NORTHCAPE 2024

DAY 4 - THREE COUNTRIES

Arvidsjaur – Alta (723 km)

As it has been a while since I last changed plans, it’s a change of plans today. Initially, I had wanted to drive the stretch up to Alta in the North of Norway in two days. But why do in two what you could do in one (very long one)? And it’ll make the last push for the Northcape on Friday a little less tiresome. The drive is going to take me from Sweden, through a short stretch of Finland, into Norway.

Due to another short night, it’s a very early start. Exactly at 4.30 am I am on the road. The morning light couldn’t be more beautiful, and as the sun rises into misty skies, the colours appear almost surreal. Santa’s gang is out and about again as well, but – contrary to the ones I come across in Norway later into my drive - on the Swedish side they at least have the decency to stand in the middle of the road so that you can see them. Strangely, the only country where you can’t see any of these buggers is Finland. Or perhaps not that strange, as this is the REAL Santa country. Perhaps there they DO take their job seriously and have started the Christmas preparations instead of wandering about on country roads hindering the tourists.

I reach Alta around 3 pm, more or less at the same time Julie arrives by plane via Oslo from Heathrow. Good bye to lonely days!
