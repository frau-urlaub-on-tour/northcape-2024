NORTHCAPE 2024

DAY 20 - LEAVING LOFOTEN

Sandoya – Glomfjord (470 km, 1 ferry crossing)

The alarm clock rings at 05.30 am and we are out of the house before 07.00 am. It’s quite a wet 2.5 hour drive to our first ferry stop in Lødingen. We are lucky to get the early ferry at 09.30 and arrive back on the mainland an hour later in Bognes.

The main highway E 6 takes us from Bognes to Fauske where we leave the main road to head for the famous Kystriksveien, the coastal road that runs almost 650 km from Bodø southwards to Steinkjer.

Our first stop is the Saltstraumen, the world’s strongest maelstrom where a huge volume of water forces its way through the 150-metre narrow strait in Saltstraumen four times a day, making enormous whirlpools that dance between the fjords. We are really lucky to get there right at the time where the tides are about to turn and the whirlpools are at their strongest.

The drive from there to our overnight stop at Glomfjord is another 2 hours with more stunning views along the route that are even more spectacular due to the rapidly changing weather from sunny to moody to rainy; all within minutes.

Our place for the night is...well….interesting. The little village of Glomfjord is mainly based around the Glomfjord hydroelectric power plant. Our (let's call it very basic) hotel has seen better days, but has a gym which is a welcome change for the muscles that haven’t been put to much use recently. And even though the restaurant doesn’t look very promising, their fish dishes are superb. And so we retire quite well fed and content at 09.30 pm.
