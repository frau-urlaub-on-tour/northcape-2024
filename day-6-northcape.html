NORTHCAPE 2024

DAY 6 AM - NORTHCAPE

Gjisvær – Northcape – Skarsvåg – Gjisvær (84 km)

We head off from Gisvær to the Northcape at 06.40 am. It’s exactly at 07.25 am that we arrive at Europe’s most northerly point. The masses have not yet invaded the place for today, so we are lucky enough to not have the views blocked by the human herds.

The rain from last night has stopped and the last remaining clouds move off to the East, too. This is the coldest I have been yet on the trip, even though the temperature is 10 degrees and the wind not too strong. I don’t want to know how that must be with gale force gusts and close to the freezing point.

After about half an hour we move on to Skarsvåg for a little hike up to a viewpoint, the Kirkeporten. It reminds us of Durdle Door in Dorset. Back home at 10.15 am, it’s coffee and breakfast time before getting the tickets for our 11.45 boat safari out to the offshore islands for some bird watching.
