NORTHCAPE 2024

DAY 5 - NORTHCAPE

Alta – Gjisvær (241 km)

After a little bit of a longer lie-in until about 6.30 am, it’s first a trip to the supermarket to get some provisions for our little house up on the Northcape before enjoying a superb breakfast in our guesthouse. After packing up, we are off to the Alta Museum, a World Heritage Rock Art Centre. As we come to learn, Alta has more rock art made by hunter-gatherers than any other place in northern Europe.

At 11 am we are on our way to the most northern part of this trip. It’s meant to be a 3,5 hour drive up to Gjisvær on the Northcape peninsula where we are renting a little Fisherman’s cottage right by the sea. However, we take much longer than that, as there is a fabulous photo stop basically around every corner.

The reindeer begin to get a bit boring as they are now appearing in the hundreds. It has to be said, though, that they are a bit better behaved than in Sweden and mostly stay out of harm’s way off the main road.

At 4 pm we finally arrive at our accomodation. It has started to rain, but we don’t care. This place is amazing in any kind of weather. And we might be lucky tomorrow morning when we will make the final push towards Europe’s most northerly point.
